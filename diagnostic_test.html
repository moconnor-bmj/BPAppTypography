Topical corticosteroids

Topical corticosteroids have been the mainstay of atopic dermatitis treatment for decades, and are the definitive standard to which all other treatments are compared. [REF 11] [C Evidence] These topical agents reduce inflammation and pruritus, and are often used in short bursts for flares of atopic dermatitis. Patients are started on low- to medium-potency topical corticosteroids and may only require intermittent use on affected areas. [C Evidence] If patients do not respond, then they may require a higher-potency corticosteroid preparation during flares and continuous use of milder forms for maintenance therapy. [REF 47] [C Evidence] [C Evidence] Again, if symptoms are not controlled, a higher-potency corticosteroid preparation may have to be used for maintenance therapy. While some guidelines recommend once daily dosing of topical corticosteroids, many of the medications are approved for twice daily dosing by the US Food and Drug Administration (FDA).
